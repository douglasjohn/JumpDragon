Travel

Fortunate to have visited 22 countries. I always have a grand time in Kuala Lumpur and I hope to visit the now second tallest building in the world,
the Merdeka 118, soon. Taipei, home to the greatest museums in the world (even better than the Smithsonian), is a remarkable city and the 101 is a
menacingly gorgeous building. Singapore, home to some of the greatest food in the world is certainly the most modern place I've been to, and quite possibly
the most modern place in the world. I still need to test this by visiting mainland China, specifically Shanghai/Hangzhou. Actually, most of my current interests
in travel can be found in mainland China. Cities like Qingdao with its magnificent shore lightshow and Chongqing's mountain city have an odd allure that
I'd love to experience. My recent summer in Abu Dhabi sparked my interest in Kuwait and Saudi Arabia, as many friends I made live there. Also, Namibia would be cool.

If I could recommend a travel place for someone, it would be Hong Kong by a mile. I solo traveled, spending 10 days there and had a blast.
The city is amazing and a blast to explore. The street food is great, the museums are phenomenal, the transit is diverse - along with the people of Hong Kong.
Although "dreamlands" like Japan exist, a major reason why I couldn't see myself living there is because the population is so homogenous. Thsi is
not the case in Hong Kong. I saw people of all colors and background while roaming the streets, and even played soccer with these kids from Nepal,
India, and Jordan. I truely connected with this city and I'm currently looking at opportunities at The University of Hong Kong. I had a blast exploring
the campus and I love the neighboring streets. Maybe post undergrad?

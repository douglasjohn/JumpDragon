Past Projects

BlackJack

Still my all-time favoite project. In highschool, I made a virtual BlackJack game with a betting system where the user
plays against the dealer. This game, made entirely in Java, was my first introduction to the possibilities of Computer Science.
I had just taken the AP Computer Science exam and thought that those ridiculous questions were what Computer Scientists did
day-to-day, but after making this game I knew it was something I wanted to pursue in some way. It actually was quite realistic,
the dealer would pull another card if it had less than the user (only if the user was at 21 or under of course), and the betting
system was quite realistic as well. Never got to implement splitting or anything like that, but I would always end up losing all
my "credits" anyways so I knew it worked and was realistic. Unfortunately, this project has been lost to the dark depths of the
dumpster when my highschool decided to spend $3.2 quadrillion to redo the whole school, including changing computers and data centers.

Eagle Scout Project

This project brought my community a new campsite that taught people how to low-impact camp. Working with the Ice Age Trail Association
this was built as an official campsite that still serves visitors today. This camp adopts the principals of Leave No Trace, ideals
Scouts hold dear. This campsite has tent pads outside of the "Bear-muda triangle" - a term deemed to the area between the cooking
area, sump, and bear bag areas. The campsite also has a sign with infografics and explanations of the camp, as well as a traffic log.

This project taught me valuable project management skills such as planning and executing multiple work days, working with local
companies to source materials (sign, sump), communicating between administrative bodies (Wisconsin DNR, Ice Age Trail Association),
and delivering on time. I also learned valuable handy man skills, all those posts, frames, and sumps don't build themselves.
